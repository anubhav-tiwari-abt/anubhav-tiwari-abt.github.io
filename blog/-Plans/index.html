2022 Plans

Getting back in the game

With the start of 2022, I'm leaving behind the rut of last 2 years (2020 and 2021).
The lockdowns and job hunts really brought down my morale a lot and I felt
like I've been stuck in a rut with my skills becoming stale and that I haven't been learning anything new,
so with the start of 2022, I've decided to take up the skills that I feel are important and that I'm lagging behind on and work on them.

C++ was my first programming language to which I was introduced to in school,
but I didn't really create anything amazing or even a decent level individual project in C++.
I've always liked the language, but my move from C++ to JavaScript and then to Python,
took me away from the core of strongly typed and closer to the metal languages.
During my career till now, I've faced situations where I felt like if I knew C++ a little better,
I'd be more up to the standards, thus, I've decided to start from scratch on C++,
going from beginner to advanced modern C++ and finally developing a project in C++.

Another skill where I found myself struggling was SQL. I mean, I know SQL, but being unable to clear 3 interviews because of SQL skill requirement, I think it's better to start working on my SQL skills before I fail another interview.

I was also unable to focus on my personal project because of several factors, and my GitHub profile was looking more full of unfinished projects and stale and abandoned projects, so I decided to go clean slate on that as well. I've some amazing new projects in mind and I wanted to start fresh on them on a fresh GitHub profile.

I tried to create a branding of an organization of myself last year, but, what started with full excitement and aspirations, ended up being nothing more than that, a branding. I'm planning to things differently this year, make something first, brand and publish it later.

Another major thing that I've been putting off for about 3 years now is a research publication, It isn't that easy, and I've in past found ways to put it on the shelf and not work on it, but I'm planning At least 1 research paper out of this year on one of my research interests.
